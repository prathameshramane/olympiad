Ranking Criteria




CRITERIA FOR RANKING- SINGLE LEVEL OLYMPIAD EXAMS:


Total marks obtained in the exam.

In case two or more students score the same number of marks, ranks will be determined on the basis of marks scored in more priority accorded sections.

In the event of a tie, marks scored by the student in the previous class might be considered to determine a rank.

In the event of two or more students scoring the same marks after considering both the above criteria, they will be awarded the same rank.

In case two or more students score same marks under the criteria above, they will be awarded the same rank.

In case of any confusion that prevails, the decision of the Academic Council will be final and binding. A telephonic round of questions may be conducted as well, to determine ranks.In case of any confusion, decision of the Academic Council will be final & binding.



CRITERIA FOR QUALIFICATION FOR 2ND LEVEL OLYMPIAD EXAMS :


Second level exams will be held for English (ISEO), Science (ISSO) and Mathematics (ISMO) Olympiads for students from class 3 onwards.


Students who fulfill either of the three following criteria will qualify for the 2nd level Olympiad exams:

Class wise, top 5% of students who appear for the 1sr level exam. Priority to marks scored in different sections will be given.

From each class, the top 25 rank holders in each zone.

The class topper from each participating school where at least 10 students from a class appear and score 50% qualifying marks



CRITERIA FOR RANKING IN 2ND LEVEL OLYMPIAD EXAM :


Ranks will be awarded according to the following criteria:

Total marks obtained in the level 2 exam.

In the event of two or more students scoring the same number of marks, ranks will be determined as followed:

Marks scored in higher priority sections in the level two exam.

On the basis of the total marks scored by the candidate in the level one exam..

Marks scored in higher priority sections in the level one exam.
