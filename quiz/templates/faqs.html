Frequently Asked Questions

Click on the below questions to solve your queries

1. Who all are eligible to sit for Olympiads?

All the students of classes 1-11 (Academic year 2020-21) are eligible to give the Final Exam.
Class 1 => Class 1 (2020-21) / Pre-primary (2019-20) (Exam for class 1 students will be parent/teacher assisted)
Class 2 => Class 2 (2020-21) / Class 1 (2019-20)
Class 3 => Class 3 (2020-21) / Class 2 (2019-20)
...
...
Class 11 => Class 11 (2020-21) / Class 10 (2019-20)

2. What will be the mode of exam?

Olympiads conducts both online and pen/paper exams. Do note that the registrations to pen/paper exam are possible only through schools.

3. Can a school register for Olympiads?
Yes, a school can register for Olympiads in any of the modes of the exam. In either case, kindly send us an email at info@olympiads.com and weâ€™ll email you the prospectus.
4.Can I register individually for Olympiads?
A student must not be denied any exam if his/her school is not participating. Hence, one of the objectives of Olympiads is to enable every student to take Olympiad exams and compete against his/her peers across India and abroad. In case, a student is unable to register for Olympiad exam through school, he/she can register for Olympiads directly.
5. Which all subjects are covered under Olympiads?
Olympiads is organised for the following subjects:
Mathematics Olympiad (UMO)
Science Olympiad (USO)
English Olympiad (UEO)
General Knowledge Olympiad (UGKO)
Cyber Olympiad (UCO)
Critical Thinking Olympiad (UCTO)
Please note that the students of Class 1 (2020-21) can register for English, Mathematics and Science only. While the students of classes 2 to 11 (2020-21) can register for all the 6 subjects.
6. How will it be possible for a student of class 1 (2020-21) to appear for online exams?
The Olympiads for class 1 students are more of a fun activity. As the kids are entering the learning phase of their life, we are trying to give them an exposure through these fun-filled exams. Do note that these exams will be parent-assisted. The duration of the exam will be of 45 minutes with 25 questions only.
7. What is Free Trial?
Free Trial is an opportunity for the students to give a full-fledged paper of Olympiads without any payment. This will help the students in understanding the exam type and hence taking the decision to register with Olympiads.
